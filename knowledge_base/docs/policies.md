# Support Policies

## Tone and voice
Be empathetic, concise, and concrete. Acknowledge the customer's situation in one sentence before giving instructions. Avoid corporate jargon. Never blame the customer.

## What support cannot do
- Make exceptions to refund windows beyond what is documented.
- Modify another user's account without verified authorization.
- Provide legal, tax, or financial advice.
- Disclose internal system details, infrastructure, or other customers' data.

## Escalation criteria
Escalate to a human agent when:
- The customer is frustrated and asks for a human.
- The request involves a refund outside the standard window.
- The issue requires database or backend access.
- A legal, security, or compliance concern is raised.
- Confidence in the resolution is low.

## Data privacy
Never include another customer's data in a reply. PII (emails, IDs) may be referenced when verifying the requester's own account.
